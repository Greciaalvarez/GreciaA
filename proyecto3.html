Bike Rental Demand Analysis

Python Analysis Project by Grecia Zarella Alvarez Leyva

Python Pandas Seaborn EDA

1. Introduction

The rental bike industry emerged strongly in 2020 as people sought safer and more flexible urban mobility. Since then, it has become a preferred option for students and professionals valuing convenience and sustainability. This project analyzes rental trends to help companies align their strategies with evolving consumer preferences and environmental shifts, identifying growth opportunities in emerging markets like Latin America.

Major Finding: Summer is the peak season with 420,714 rentals, while Winter shows the lowest demand at 86,785 rentals.

2. Methodology

This end-to-end analysis was conducted using a dataset of 1,387 records. The process included:

  • Data Wrangling: Cleaning and structuring the dataset using Pandas.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Utilizing Seaborn and Matplotlib to visualize correlations between weather factors (temperature, humidity, wind) and total rentals.
  • Feature Correlation: Identifying that Temperature has a moderate positive correlation (0.55) with rental volume.

3. Key Insights & Visualization

Commuter Behavior

Peak demand occurs at 8:00 AM and between 5:00-6:00 PM, indicating that the primary user base consists of daily commuters.

Weather Impact

Rainfall has a negative correlation (-0.11), while high temperatures and low rainfall are ideal drivers for demand.

4. Actionable Strategy

Based on the data-driven results, the following steps are recommended:

  • Operational Focus: Prioritize fleet availability and marketing during the Summer, especially in June.
  • Product Offering: Market weekday supply and value-priced commuter packages for routine riders.
  • Expansion: Invest in infrastructure and tourist campaigns in underpenetrated markets, primarily in Latin America.

5. Conclusion

Bicycle rental demand is driven by commuting patterns, tourism, and seasonal factors. By leveraging data to understand shifting consumer behavior, companies can position themselves for long-term growth and successful entry into new geographical markets.
