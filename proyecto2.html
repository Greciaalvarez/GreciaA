1. Project Overview

This study evaluates the development and performance of two bullish strategies: the Bull Call Spread and the Covered Call. By utilizing Python to process real option chain data, the analysis identifies optimal entry points based on market sentiment and risk-reward profiles.

2. Implied Volatility Analysis

The Volatility Smile: To understand pricing efficiency, I modeled Implied Volatility (IV) across strikes. The analysis revealed a clear skew, indicating market expectations for future price movements.

5. Conclusion

The quantitative analysis demonstrates that while the Covered Call offers a significantly higher probability of success (61.97%), the Bull Call Spread is the more capital-efficient tool for targeted growth with limited downside. By automating the cleaning of 14 market variables and modeling "The Greeks," this project proves that data-driven insights are essential for navigating complex derivatives markets and optimizing portfolio management.
