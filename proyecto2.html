1. Executive Overview

This study evaluates two high-conviction bullish strategies: the Bull Call Spread and the Covered Call. The analysis focuses on contract selection through liquidity filtering, risk/reward profiling using "The Greeks," and performance optimization based on the current market environment.

2. Methodology: Data Integrity

To ensure real-world trade execution, the raw option chain data was filtered for liquidity. Contracts with a Bid/Ask spread > 0.01 and Open Interest < 5 were excluded to eliminate theoretical gains that lack market depth.

The Core Factor: Implied Volatility (IV)

A critical step in the analysis was modeling the IV Smile. By visualizing Implied Volatility against Strike Prices, I identified the market's demand for protection. This skew was essential in determining the cost-efficiency of the Bull Call Spread vs. the income potential of the Covered Call.
