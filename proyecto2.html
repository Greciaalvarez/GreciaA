1. Project Scope

This project applies quantitative finance concepts to evaluate two bullish options strategies: Bull Call Spread and Covered Call. Using real market data, the analysis covers the entire pipeline from data cleaning (liquidity filtering) to advanced profitability modeling and risk sensitivity analysis (The Greeks).

2. Quantitative Methodology

Data Cleaning: To ensure trade feasibility, I filtered the dataset for liquidity. Only contracts with an Open Interest > 5 and a Bid/Ask spread > 0.01 were considered to avoid "paper gains" that cannot be executed in real markets.

Implied Volatility Smile: Analyzed the IV skew, identifying higher premiums in out-of-the-money (OTM) put options, reflecting market hedging sentiment.

3. Comparative Strategy Analysis

Using Python, I modeled the Profit/Loss (P&L) diagrams for both strategies based on their probability of profit and cost efficiency.

5. Professional Conclusion

The analysis concludes that while the Covered Call offers a higher statistical probability of profit, the Bull Call Spread provides superior risk-adjusted returns for traders with limited capital. This project demonstrates the ability to translate complex financial derivatives into actionable investment data.
