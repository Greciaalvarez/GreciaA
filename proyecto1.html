1. Research Framework

This independent research and development project focuses on the creation of an algorithmic system designed to analyze financial markets and evaluate trading risk through quantitative rules and systematic backtesting. The objective of the project is to translate discretionary trading concepts into a structured, rule-based algorithm that can identify potential trading opportunities under defined market conditions.

2. System Architecture

The system evolved from a Python-based research prototype into a production-level Expert Advisor (EA) developed in MetaTrader 5 (MQL5). The architecture is designed to minimize emotional bias and maximize algorithmic discipline:

  • Directional Bias (H4 timeframe): Market trend is defined using EMA (20/50) crossovers to capture higher timeframe momentum.
  • Execution Layer (H1 timeframe): Trade entries are triggered using a Pullback and Resumption model, filtered by RSI (momentum) and ATR (volatility).
  • Risk Management Engine:
    • Dynamic position sizing based on account equity.
    • ATR-based stop-loss to adapt to market volatility.
    • A fixed risk-reward ratio to ensure consistency across trades.

This multi-layered structure separates context, confirmation, and execution, improving decision consistency.

3. Core Engineering Snippet

The following snippet illustrates how volatility-adjusted risk is applied through ATR-based stop-loss and dynamic position sizing:

// ATR-Based Stop Loss & Position Sizing Engine
double sl = structuralLow - (atr * 1.60);
double lot = CalculateLotSize(MathAbs(close1 - sl)/_Point);
double tp = close1 + (MathAbs(close1 - sl) * 2);
OpenTradeBuy(lot, sl, tp);

This logic ensures that every trade is executed under predefined risk constraints, eliminating emotional decision-making and enforcing consistency.
