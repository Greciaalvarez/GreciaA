Automated Trading System (ATS)

Quantitative Engineering & Algorithmic Strategy | Forex

1. Research Premise: Context vs. Permission

This system was built upon a quantitative investigation into multi-timeframe analysis. Inspired by Brian Shannon's market theory, I challenged the assumption that strict alignment across all timeframes is necessary. My research suggests that higher timeframes define market context, while lower timeframes provide the tactical entry. Enforcing total alignment often results in "analysis paralysis" or missed volatility; this system balances institutional context with agile execution.

2. Technical Architecture

The transition from a research prototype (Python) to an automated engine (MQL5) required a move from data manipulation to event-driven programming. The system utilizes an event-driven architecture that responds to real-time market ticks, ensuring latency-sensitive execution.

Engineering Highlights:

  • Structural Logic: Utilizes H4 EMA trends to establish directional bias, preventing trades against the institutional current.
  • Volatility-Adjusted Risk: Replaced static Stop Losses with an ATR (Average True Range) buffer ($1.72 \times ATR$), allowing the system to expand risk parameters during high volatility.
  • Automated Risk Governance: A dynamic lot-size calculator that adapts to account equity on every trade, maintaining a strict risk percentage regardless of account growth or drawdown.

3. Execution Methodology (MQL5 Snippet)

// Dynamic Risk Management & ATR Buffer Integration double sl = structuralLow - (atr * 1.72); double lot = CalculateLotSize(MathAbs(close1 - sl)/_Point); double tp = close1 + (MathAbs(close1 - sl) * 2); // Execute trade if all quantitative thresholds are met OpenTradeBuy(lot, sl, tp);

4. Outcome & Insights

The system proves that automated trading is not merely about signal generation—it is about execution discipline. By quantifying risk management into the core codebase, the system effectively mitigates the "human element," ensuring that every trade strictly adheres to the established Quantitative Rules regardless of market volatility.
